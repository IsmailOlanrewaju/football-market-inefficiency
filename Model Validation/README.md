## Model Validation

To evaluate the reliability of the statistical valuation framework, several diagnostic tests were conducted before interpreting player market value estimates.

### 1. Multicollinearity Testing

Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) analysis was performed for each positional model to ensure that selected performance metrics were not excessively correlated.

The final models maintained acceptable VIF levels, suggesting that the chosen variables provided distinct information rather than measuring the same underlying attribute repeatedly.

---

### 2. Residual Analysis

Residual diagnostics were performed to examine whether the regression assumptions were reasonably satisfied.

The following tests were applied:

- Breusch-Pagan test for heteroscedasticity
- Shapiro-Wilk test for residual normality
- Cook's Distance to identify influential observations

These tests helped identify potential limitations caused by extreme market valuations, elite reputation effects, and unusual player profiles.

---

### 3. Out-of-Sample Prediction Framework

The models were designed as explanatory valuation tools rather than perfect transfer fee predictors.

Predicted market values were compared against observed market values to measure:

- Where the statistical model agrees with current market perception
- Where player valuations appear higher or lower than expected based on performance and profile indicators

The objective was not to identify "correct prices" but to measure deviations between statistical indicators and market expectations.

---

### 4. Positional Model Validation

Because player valuation differs significantly by position, separate models were developed:

- Defender valuation model
- Midfielder valuation model
- Forward valuation model

Each model used position-specific performance metrics rather than applying a universal formula across all players.

Examples:

**Defenders**
- Defensive actions per 90
- Tackles won
- Recoveries
- Dispossessions

**Midfielders**
- Ball recoveries
- Interceptions
- Chance creation
- Expected assists

**Forwards**
- Expected goals
- Shots on target
- Dribbling output
- Expected assists

This positional approach reduced bias from comparing fundamentally different player roles.

---

### 5. Interpretation of Results

The model outputs should be interpreted as statistical estimates of market alignment, not definitive transfer valuations.

Football transfer prices are influenced by additional factors that are difficult to quantify, including:

- Club reputation
- Contract situations
- International profile
- Commercial value
- Future potential
- Negotiation dynamics

Therefore, the project focuses on understanding market behaviour and identifying valuation patterns rather than replacing professional scouting or recruitment processes.
